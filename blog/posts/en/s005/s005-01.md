I never really thought of my voice that much.

For the most part, I've treated it the same way I would treat something like a hammer: a tool to get the job done. I've used it to talk, ask questions, make jokes, and (attempt to) sing if the situation called for it (which is thankfully not often). 

Occasionally, I may get a comment on it, positive or negative, but I never thought of it as something to train or study. At least not seriously. It was just my voice and how I sound.

But now I would say over the last year and a half, my perspective has shifted. 

The way I sound affects how clearly I communicate, how confident I seem, how comfortable I feel speaking in front of people, and even how well I can learn another language. My voice isn't just sound coming out of my mouth. It's part of how I present myself to the world.

## More Than Sound

There's a really interesting metaphor used by communication expert and YouTuber, Vihn Giang: "Your voice is an instrument."
